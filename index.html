Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about eye health, examinations, and consultation fees.

It is recommended to get an eye examination once every 1–2 years. People who frequently use screens or have pre-existing conditions like diabetes or high blood pressure should visit every 6–12 months.
Visual problems, persistent eye pain, redness, irritation, excessive watering, sudden blurry vision, flashes of light, floaters, or frequent headaches should be checked by an ophthalmologist immediately.
Eye doctors who specialize in medical and surgical eye care are called Ophthalmologists. Those who specialize in vision testing and corrective lenses are called Optometrists.
Consultation charges may vary depending on the diagnosis and treatment plan needed. Patients can contact the clinic directly via phone or WhatsApp for specific consultation fee structures.
